For Faculty

Library Instruction Sessions

    The Library provides in-class instructional sessions that teach students about the Library's resources and services. Subjects covered:

    • doing research at the college level

    • effectively searching, locating, and evaluating information

    • popular and scholarly resources relevant to a specific assignment

    • basic and advanced searching of library resources, such as the catalog and article databases

    • discipline-specific resources and scholarly communication within that discipline


    To schedule an library instruction session please contact us at: library@mcny.edu.

Faculty Orientation and Research Consultations

The Library offers an orientation for all faculty and staff who request it. Includes an overview of sources available through the library, and provide tips for conducting effective research on any topic. One on one research consultations are also available. To set up an library orientation or research consultation email the library at library@mcny.edu.

The Audrey Cohen Archive

MCNY started out all the way back in 1964, as the brainchild of educational pioneer Audrey Cohen. From its small beginnings as her Women's Talent Corps, the College grew to the vital and thriving educational institution that it is today. You can make an appointment to explore its history using the library's Audrey Cohen Archive. It contains material from the early days, through our years as Audrey Cohen College, to the present day as Metroplitan College of New York.
