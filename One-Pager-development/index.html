For Students

  • One on One Research Help
    • You can meet one-on-one with a reference librarian to learn about MCNY's databases, or to work on a specific research question. You can come to the reference desk in person during normal library hours with a question;
    Call 212.343.1234 ext. 2008/2010;
    Or via email: library@mcny.edu, putting "Reference Question" in the subject line. You will be contacted by email within 24 business hours (Monday-Saturday) after you submit your request.

  • Technology Tutoring
    • The MCNY Library Staff also offer tutoring for the following software programs: Microsoft Word; Microsoft Powerpoint; Microsoft Excel. Please contact us at library@mcny.edu , and put "Request for Tech Tutoring" in the subject line.

    For Faculty

  • Library Instruction Sessions
    • The Library provides in-class instructional sessions that teach students about the Library's resources and services. These sessions aim to enhance the academic experience of MCNY students, to improve their research skills, and to foster information literacy and critical thinking. The Library encourages faculty in all departments to schedule library instruction sessions for their Constructive Action courses. Together we can introduce your students to:
      • doing research at the college level
      • effectively searching, locating, and evaluating information
      • popular and scholarly resources relevant to a specific assignment
      • basic and advanced searching of library resources, such as the catalog and article databases
      • discipline-specific resources and scholarly communication within that discipline

      To schedule an library instruction session please contact us at: library@mcny.edu We look forward to working with you and your students.

  • Faculty Orientation and Research Consultations
    • As a part of the MCNY community, you have access to a tremendous amount of material through the library's online subscription databases and print collection. In addition, we provide research help, interlibrary loan, and other services. There's a lot of material out there, and navigating it – while simultaneously helping students to as well – can be tricky. Therefore, the Library offers an orientation for all faculty and staff who request it. The orientation will provide an overview of sources available through the library, and provide tips for conducting effective research on any topic. Librarians are here to support faculty research and teaching and as well as student work. One on one research consultations are also available. Sessions can be tailored to any subject or level of expertise. To set up an library orientation or research consultation email the library at library@mcny.edu.

    How To Use The MCNY Library

    Who can use the MCNY Library?

    MCNY students, MCNY faculty, and MCNY staff

    What do you need to take out a book?

    A current MCNY student or faculty/staff ID.

    How do I register?

    Bring your ID into the library. Ask a staff member for a registration form, and fill it out. That's it!

    How do I use the MCNY library's online materials?

    You can use them from any computer, just by logging in with the same username and password you use with Moodle.

    Interlibrary Loan

    Interlibrary Loan is used as a way of expanding library resources by obtaining material from other libraries. If you are a student or member of the faculty or staff at MCNY and you need something that is not owned by our library, you may be able to obtain it from another using this service. Books, electronic articles not available through our dababases, and photocopies of journal articles are examples of material available through Interlibrary Loan.
    On average, it takes at least 7-10 days to fill an ILL book request, and 1-2 days for journal articles. Loan periods and renewal options are determined by the lending library.
    If you are interested in requesting an item via Interlibrary loan contact Kate Adler, Reference Librarian at via email: kadler@mcny.edu
